Aegis Frontier Capital

Core belief
In defense and national security, software alone is insufficient and hardware alone is obsolete. The winning companies tightly integrate hardware, software, and AI into systems that work under real-world constraints where failure is not an option.

What we believe
• Modern defense advantage comes from systems, not components
• Software defines adaptability, hardware defines survivability
• Onboard intelligence and edge autonomy are mandatory, not optional
• Vertically integrated teams move faster than primes and out-execute pure software vendors in contested environments

Focus
Early and growth-stage companies building full-stack defense technologies, including
• Autonomous air, ground, and maritime platforms
• Edge compute, perception, and control software tightly coupled to hardware
• Mission-critical software that operates disconnected, degraded, or denied
• Fleet-level orchestration, health monitoring, and lifecycle management

We prioritize platforms that can ship, deploy, and iterate with operators in the loop, not lab-bound technology.

Stage
Seed through Series B, with willingness to underwrite hardware risk early when technical integration is the moat and software leverage compounds over time.

Accept
• Higher upfront capital intensity
• Longer development timelines
• Regulatory and procurement friction

In exchange for
• Durable defensibility
• Strategic relevance to defense buyers
• Long-lived platforms that compound across programs and domains

Avoid
• Software divorced from physical reality
• Hardware without continuous software advantage
• Single-program science projects
